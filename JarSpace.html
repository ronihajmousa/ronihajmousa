JarSpace - Housing Made Simple

Jarspace was a personal-driven project which was inspired by the challenge around young adults (18-25) seeking for rental accommodation. The goal was to create a brand & product that is contradictory to traditional flat-hunting methods avoiding a corporate outlook with letting agencies commonly found within existing applications such as Rightmove, Zoopla, or OnTheMarket. Unlike existing apps, educative and informative features were also included within the app alongside property searching to create an enjoyable and worthwhile experience but also to give young adults a better understanding about the moving-in process and what to expect.

A mobile application was designed with implementation of the double diamond process. With one diamond representing the early phase of discovering what exists in the sector regarding digital property services. As well as discovering who the intended target demographic are and defining their problems in relation to accommadation experience. This led on to the second diamond of beginning to understand user journeys and potential solutions for the focus group. With implementation of unorthodox features not commonly found in existing applications such as RightMove. These features provide the likes of an gamified but educative experience using digital flashcards to test housing knowledge and a sense of personalization with avatar profiles and including hobbies and interests for other users to see.

Alongside the prototype, branding guidelines were designed. This included a logo, visual shapes, a colour scheme and typography. The objective when creating the stylesheet was how can we make this application stand out from the traditional property providers that already exist? Adjectives to best describe the brand was the fundamental key to beginning developing visual assets. Rather than using shades of blue with a mundane logotype to represent trust, shades of purple, pink, red and light orange were better fitted for the 'anti-corporate' theme. This was also to symbolise the 'magical' experience of moving out of their parents for the first time. Corrolating alongside the unicorn logo emblem with connotations of fantasy and magic.
